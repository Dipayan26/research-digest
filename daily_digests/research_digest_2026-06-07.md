TOPIC TAG: Clinical ML

Its retrospective virtual screening enrichment on the ROBIN benchmark shows early enrichment gains that directly translate to hit-identification campaigns for RNA-targeted therapeutics, which are particularly relevant as ~85% of human proteins lack small-molecule binding pockets.

TOPIC TAG: Clinical ML

The traceable decision log and explicit risk flags make outputs interpretable to medicinal chemists, enabling direct integration into early-stage target assessment workflows rather than black-box scoring.

TITLE: Retrieval and Competition: How a Protein Foundation Model Starts a Protein
AUTHORS: Piotr Jedryszek, Oliver M. Crook
SOURCE: arXiv | 2605.16331 | [**Link**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16331)
TOPIC TAG: Protein LM
TLDR: Analysis of the token-by-token generation dynamics of an autoregressive protein language model reveals that the model begins each sequence through a retrieval-like search over its training distribution, with early-position tokens competing via a sharp softmax to lock in a taxonomic class and domain-family archetype before committing to residue-level generation.
WHY IT MATTERS: Understanding how pLMs begin a sequence generation is foundational to controlled protein design: if the model "retrieves" a fold class implicitly in the first few tokens, then conditioning at generation time must happen prior to this commitment or risk being overridden. These mechanistic insights from the generation dynamics parallel analogous findings in natural language models (topic priming, conceptual anchoring) but are now established specifically for the protein sequence domain, informing prefix-tuning, constrained decoding, and scaffold-seeding strategies.
