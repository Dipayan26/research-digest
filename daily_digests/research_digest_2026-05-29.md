### 1. PlasmidLM: A Promptable DNA Language Model via Verifiable-Reward Post-Training
**Why this is the highlight:** PlasmidLM is the first generative DNA model that accepts plain-English component specifications and generates complete, multi-kilobase plasmid constructs in a single autoregressive pass—achieving 89.7% useful-plasmid rate under best-of-4 sampling. The key innovation is verifiable-reward post-training: using structural plausibility (is it a valid circular DNA?) and component faithfulness (do detected parts match the prompt?) as objective rewards in an RLHF-like loop, rather than subjective preference labels. This directly translates the LLM reasoning alignment playbook into synthetic biology, setting a new paradigm for prompt-driven DNA design that could dramatically accelerate plasmid construction in wet labs.

### 2. CHANRG: Fair Splits Flip the Leaderboard for RNA Secondary-Structure Prediction
**Why this is the highlight:** CHANRG is a landmark benchmarking study using 170k structure-diverse, non-redundant RNAs with genome-aware splits and structure-aware deduplication. The headline finding is alarming: the rankings of 29 RNA structure predictors completely flip when data leakage is removed—RNA foundation models that appeared state-of-the-art drop dramatically out-of-distribution, while simpler structured decoders prove more robust. This is a cautionary result for the entire genomic foundation model community, revealing that benchmark contamination has been systematically overestimating generalization in RNA structure prediction for years.
**Code/Weights:** Not released

### 3. ELF: Embedded Language Flows
**Why this is the highlight:** ELF introduces a fundamentally new design principle for diffusion/flow-based sequence generation: operate in continuous embedding space throughout the entire generation process, and only discretize at the very last step using a shared-weight network. This eliminates the quality bottleneck caused by early mapping to discrete tokens in all existing diffusion language models. ELF substantially outperforms both discrete (D3PM-style) and continuous diffusion LMs with significantly fewer sampling steps, and the approach directly ports to protein and DNA sequence generation via protein LM embedding spaces—making it one of the most practically relevant architecture innovations this month.
