TITLE: Plug-and-Play Guidance for Discrete Diffusion Models via Gradient-Informed Logit Correction
AUTHORS: Hongkun Dou, Zike Chen, Fengji Li, Hongjue Li, Yue Deng
SOURCE: arXiv | 2606.06303 | [**Link**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06303)
TOPIC TAG: Diffusion
TLDR: GILC (Gradient-Informed Logit Correction) introduces a Jacobian-free, training-free plug-and-play guidance mechanism for discrete diffusion models that directly corrects clean-prediction logits using gradient-informed signals, achieving SOTA performance on DNA, protein sequence, and molecular generation tasks without any fine-tuning.
WHY IT MATTERS: Classifier guidance has been transformative for continuous diffusion in vision, but discrete sequence diffusion has lacked efficient analogues because gradients through categorical tokens are undefined; GILC's Jacobian-free design bypasses this by operating on the output logits of a variational proxy, making any pre-trained discrete diffusion model (DPLM, MDLM, LLaDA variants) instantly steerable by arbitrary reward functions including non-differentiable wet-lab oracles. The finding that GILC consistently outperforms fine-tuning approaches at inference time is directly actionable for protein and DNA design campaigns where retraining budgets are limited and new design objectives emerge continuously.

TITLE: Riemannian MeanFlow: Few-Step Protein Backbone and DNA Generation on Manifolds
AUTHORS: Dongyeop Woo, Marta Skreta, Seonghyun Park, Kirill Neklyudov, Sungsoo Ahn
SOURCE: arXiv | 2602.07744 | [**Link**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07744)
TOPIC TAG: Flow Matching
TLDR: Riemannian MeanFlow (RMF) learns generative flow maps directly on Riemannian manifolds through three equivalent characterizations of manifold average velocity (Eulerian, Lagrangian, semigroup), enabling protein backbone generation and promoter DNA design in as few as one forward pass—up to 10× fewer function evaluations than prior flow methods with comparable sample quality.
WHY IT MATTERS: Protein backbone generators and DNA design models based on flow matching or diffusion require tens to hundreds of neural function evaluations at inference time, creating a computational bottleneck that limits large-scale in silico protein engineering and virtual library screening; RMF directly learns the flow map rather than approximating it through many denoising steps, collapsing generation to 1–5 evaluations without retraining from scratch. The reward look-ahead feature—where terminal states are predicted from intermediate steps at minimal additional cost—further enables efficient reward-guided generation, combining the sample efficiency of flow maps with the ability to bias toward high-fitness sequences.
