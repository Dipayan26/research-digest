### 2. Riemannian MeanFlow: Few-Step Generative Modeling on Manifolds
**Why this is the highlight:** Inference efficiency is the central deployment bottleneck for protein and DNA generative models. Riemannian MeanFlow learns flow maps directly on Riemannian manifolds by deriving three equivalent characterizations of manifold average velocity (Eulerian, Lagrangian, semigroup), achieving comparable sample quality to leading diffusion/flow models with up to 10× fewer forward passes on protein backbone and DNA promoter generation. Crucially, the few-step nature of RMF also enables reward look-ahead—predicting terminal states from intermediate generation steps at near-zero additional cost—providing a clean mechanism for reward-guided and property-conditioned design that bypasses expensive full trajectory rollouts.
