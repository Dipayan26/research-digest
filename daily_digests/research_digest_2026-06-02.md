AUTHORS: Dongyeop Woo, Marta Skreta, Seonghyun Park, Kirill Neklyudov, Sungsoo Ahn
SOURCE: HF Papers | 2602.07744 | [**Link**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07744)
TOPIC TAG: Flow Matching
TLDR: Riemannian MeanFlow (RMF) learns flow maps directly on Riemannian manifolds—enabling high-quality one-forward-pass generation—by deriving three equivalent characterizations of manifold average velocity (Eulerian, Lagrangian, and semigroup) and applying them to promoter DNA design and protein backbone generation, achieving up to 10x fewer function evaluations than prior flow methods.
WHY IT MATTERS: Current flow and diffusion models for proteins and DNA require tens to hundreds of neural network calls at inference, creating a bottleneck in high-throughput design pipelines; RMF directly learns the terminal mapping rather than the instantaneous velocity field, collapsing multi-step integration to one or few steps without quality degradation. The reward look-ahead mechanism—predicting terminal states from intermediate steps—further enables efficient reward-guided design, making RMF a practical accelerator for closed-loop generative biology workflows.
