TITLE: When Does Structure Help? The Information Bonus of AlphaFold2 Representations over Protein Language Models

TOPIC TAG: Protein LM

TITLE: DeltaDiff: Training-Free, Physics-Guided Machine Learning for Predicting Mutant Protein Structures

SOURCE: arXiv | 2606.04452 | [**Link**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04452)
TOPIC TAG: Protein LM
TLDR: DeltaDiff injects mutation-aware physical guidance into the inference trajectory of a pre-trained protein structure diffusion model without any fine-tuning, enabling accurate prediction of nonlocal conformational changes induced by single-site or few-site mutations in cases where wild-type and mutant structures diverge substantially.

Accurate nonlocal mutant structure prediction is a critical bottleneck in variant effect interpretation, directed evolution, and PROTAC degrader design—where small mutations shift degradability through global conformational rearrangements.

TLDR: Low-Pass Flow Matching replaces the white-noise source of standard flow matching with an operator-modulated interpolant that induces a time-varying spectral bias transitioning from the source spectrum to a frequency-decaying bias near the data—aligning the generative path with the power spectral structure of natural data distributions.
WHY IT MATTERS: Standard flow matching sources are spectrally flat (white noise), creating a mismatch with biological data distributions (protein conformational ensembles, genomic sequences, molecular property landscapes) that exhibit strong low-frequency structure; the operator-modulated interpolant is a principled, drop-in replacement for any FM model that improves generation quality on data with natural frequency decay.
