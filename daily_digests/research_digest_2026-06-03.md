TOPIC TAG: Diffusion
TLDR: By replacing Gaussian noise with a Student's t-distribution and deriving an SDE-based sampler with a state-dependent diffusion coefficient, this work introduces self-regulating annealing that adaptively modulates effective noise scale, achieving substantially improved tail fidelity on heavy-tailed biological and scientific datasets.
WHY IT MATTERS: Standard Gaussian diffusion models systematically underfit the heavy tails of molecular property distributions, genomic variant effect sizes, and fitness landscapes—leading to mode collapse and missed rare-but-functionally-important sequences; the Student's t-based forward process provides a principled, practically simple replacement that requires no architectural change to existing diffusion models. The self-regulating annealing mechanism is parameter-free, making it a drop-in upgrade for any existing protein or DNA diffusion model trained on biologically realistic (non-Gaussian) data distributions.

**#3 — Gumbel-Softmax Flow Matching with Straight-Through Guidance (STGFlow)**
arXiv 2503.17361 | [**Link**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.17361)

STGFlow introduces a genuinely novel generative framework for biological sequences that scales discrete flow matching to protein-length alphabets—a key limitation of all prior simplex-based flow matching methods—through a time-dependent temperature Gumbel-Softmax interpolant that smoothly anneals from a soft categorical distribution to a one-hot vertex. The Straight-Through Guided Flows mechanism provides training-free, classifier-based guidance using straight-through estimators, enabling efficient inference-time steering with any pre-trained sequence classifier—not requiring structural oracles or reward model training. State-of-the-art performance across three distinct bio-design tasks (DNA promoter design, protein sequence generation, rare-disease target-binding peptide design) validates the approach as a broadly applicable conditional generation engine for the biological sequence space.
